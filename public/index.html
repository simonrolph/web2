Hi, I’m Simon Rolph

I’m a PhD student at the University of Sheffield.

I love wildlife. I also like coding, and steam trains. I’m a huge fan of botanical gardens and I have a terrible habit of taking lots of pictures of plants and never getting around to identifying them.

So, what do I study?

Background: we observe an impressive diversity of life history strategies across the tree of life.

Opportunity: decades of population biology has resulted in data on the demography of hundreds of plants and animal species.

My research: how can we use this demographic data, alongside a plethora of population modelling techniques, to ask fundamental and applied questions about life history strategies?
