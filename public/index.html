Hi, I’m Simon Rolph

I’m a PhD student at the University of Sheffield.

I love wildlife. I also like coding, and steam trains. I’m a huge fan of botanical gardens and I have a terrible habit of taking lots of pictures of plants and never getting around to identifying them.

So, what do I study?

We observe an impressive diversity of life history strategies across the tree of life.

Decades of population biology has resulted in data on the demography of hundreds of plants and animal species.

How can we use this demographic data, alongside a plethora of population modelling techniques, to ask fundamental questions about life history trade-offs?

Communicating science!

I have a irregularly updated youtube channel with such hits as “Life in a Jar” and “Cornish Wildflowers on Rosemullion Head”. Pretty thrilling.
